Overall theme

The podcast discusses the importance of servanthood and delegation within the church, using the example of the seven deacons from Acts 6. It highlights how the early church faced challenges in caring for its growing community and how they addressed these issues by empowering others to serve. The message encourages listeners to recognise their own calling and the value of every role in the body of Christ, emphasising that no task is too small or menial. Ultimately, it calls for a spirit of obedience and willingness to step into one's gifts for the benefit of the community.

Key quotations

  • “Delegating is a key skill in preventing burnout, getting tasks completed and enabling others to be released into their use of their talents and skills.”
  • “Every role in the body of Christ, every gift is required so that the body can function, so we can keep the body of Christ healthy and well.”
  • “God can do so much more than we think.”

Bible passages

  • Acts 6:1-7
  • Ephesians 3:20

Questions you may wish to reflect on

  • What are some practical ways to delegate tasks in a community setting?
  • How can we identify our spiritual gifts and calling?
  • What role does servanthood play in our daily lives?
  • How can we support those who are stepping into new ministries?
  • What challenges do we face in including diverse groups within our communities?

Further reading

  • 1 Timothy 3:8-13 — This passage provides further insight into the qualifications and responsibilities of deacons, illustrating the development of church leadership roles over time.
  • Matthew 20:26-28 — This passage highlights Jesus' teaching on servanthood and leadership, reinforcing the idea that true greatness comes from serving others.
